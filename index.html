About Me

Since I was a kid, my mind would always wonder about how anything and everything worked. That concept remained consistent throughout my life, applying it to more and more topics as I came across them. Throughout high school, my curiosity shifted towards understanding how computers work, specifically the relationship between hardware and software. Diving more into that led me to be where I am right now; a second-year Computer Engineering student at the University of Waterloo.

My fascination with cars has been a significant part of me since my childhood. It started off as admiring fast-looking sports cars and then expanded to loving motorsports. Now my love for cars has further expanded to understanding the brilliant engineering behind a car. I enjoy learning about the mechanical engineering aspects of cars and engines during my spare time, applying my learnings on my own car and sometimes my friend's cars. The Computer Engineer side of me enjoys learning about the hardware and software in modern cars. Currently, I am focusing on learning about how autonomous vehicles work. I recently completed an introductory course on Self Driving Cars. From this course, I was able to learn a lot about the hardware and software stack, as well as various models. Through that learning, I was able to implement a working self-driving model.

Wondering about how anything and everything works continues into my personal life too. Apart from working on projects relating to topics I am passionate about. I like to read non-fiction books, and I enjoy listening to podcasts. One of my favourite podcasts is the “Huberman Lab” which discusses how the human brain works. Apart from that, I enjoy watching hockey, basketball, and especially Formula 1!
